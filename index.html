What I've Built

AI at CENTINA

When I started using AI tools for my estimating work at CENTINA, a $70M construction company in South Australia, the productivity gains were immediate. I wasn't just saving time on individual tasks — I was changing how work got done. That caught attention, and I was promoted to Head of AI.

I wrote the board paper proposing a $76,600 investment in company-wide AI adoption. I built detailed SOPs for every role — site supervisors, commercial engineers, contract administrators, estimators. I developed internal tools including pipeline forecasting systems, project health dashboards, and subcontractor recommendation engines. The work wasn't theoretical. It was about taking real construction workflows and making them dramatically faster without sacrificing quality.

PromptAI Consulting

The experience at CENTINA became the foundation for PromptAI, my AI implementation service for construction companies and SME builders. Most construction companies know they should be using AI, but adoption doesn't stick without structured implementation, hands-on training, and ongoing accountability.

PromptAI delivers three things: an AI readiness audit that identifies where AI will actually move the needle, a hands-on implementation program with role-specific training on real work, and a monthly retainer that keeps teams current as the tools evolve. I've built out the platform and have a design partner live.
