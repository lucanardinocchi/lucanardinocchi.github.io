About

Who I Am

I'm a double-degree student at the University of Sydney studying aerospace engineering and philosophy. That combination isn't accidental. Aerospace gives me the technical foundation: dynamics, control systems, computational methods, structures, aerodynamics. Philosophy gives me the thinking tools: how to reason clearly under uncertainty, how to evaluate competing frameworks, and how to ask the right questions when the problem isn't well-defined.

I grew up in Adelaide with strong family connections to the construction industry, which led me to CENTINA, a $70M builder in South Australia, where I work as a Residential Estimator. That role became the starting point for everything else.

At 18, I moved to Sydney by myself — no college, no contacts, a $200-a-week stipend. I built financial stability from nothing, managed every part of my life myself, and figured out the rest as I went. I've been doing that ever since.

What I Do Outside Uni

I work as Residential Estimator and Head of AI at CENTINA, a $70M construction company in South Australia, where I led AI adoption across 24 office staff and authored a $76,600 board-level investment proposal for company-wide AI implementation. I also run PromptAI Consulting, an AI implementation service for construction companies and SME builders, with a design partner live and productivity improvements ranging from 50% to 400% on AI-assisted tasks.

I've built a personal management system that runs my calendar autonomously and a personal CRM to maintain all my relationships. I'm deeply AI-native — I don't use AI as a tool bolted onto my workflow, it is my workflow.

I'm building a robotics community in Sydney — organising meets, projects, and workshops with a small, high-performance group. The goal is a space for people who want to build real systems, not just talk about them.

I volunteer at Double Bay Library doing tech support and tutor low-income students at Weave in Woolloomooloo. I grew up watching Friday and every African-American comedy I could get my hands on. I don't take myself too seriously — I take the work seriously.

I hike, go to the beach, boulder and run almost every day, box, play futsal, and get out spearfishing and sailing when I can. Staying physical keeps the rest of it sharp.

Where I'm Heading

My interests are converging on autonomous systems, robotics, applied AI, and defense technology. I want to have fun and win in frontier tech.

I'm drawn to European defense tech in particular. The continent is in the middle of its biggest military investment surge since the Cold War, and the startups emerging in this space are working on genuinely hard problems — GPS-denied navigation, autonomous maritime platforms, computer vision for FPV drones, AI-powered command-and-control. These are small teams building things that matter, under real constraints, with real consequences.

Long-term, I see myself working at the intersection of engineering, AI, and strategy — in a place where the technical work is demanding, the team is small and trusted, and the stakes are real. I work really hard. I want to be where the people are fun and driven, and we're building something that matters.
